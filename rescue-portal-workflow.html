STEP 4

Resident Submits SOS

rescue-portal.ph/resident
👤
Good evening, Juan
🆘
Request Rescue
Tap to send emergency alert
🚨 SEND SOS ALERT
🚨
GPS must be enabled! The browser will ask for location permission. If denied, the resident must manually provide an address. GPS is critical for dispatch accuracy.
⚠️
Emergency Type is required. Options: Fire, Flood, Medical, Earthquake, Vehicular Accident, Crime, Typhoon, Landslide, and more.
📝
Description helps responders prepare. Be specific: how many people, floor/location, hazards. Even a short sentence helps.
⚠️
Severity levels: Low (non-urgent), Medium (help needed soon), High (urgent, possible injuries), Critical (life-threatening, immediate response).
✓
After submission: Incident status = "submitted." A reference number is generated (e.g., INC-20260621-0042). The resident can track progress in real time from their dashboard.
🚫
FALSE ALERTS: Filing fake emergencies will result in immediate account suspension. This wastes critical rescue resources.

⚠️
Verify before dispatching! Click "Call" to confirm with the reporter. Check if the location pin makes sense. Mark as verified only if confirmed real.

✓
Status flow here: submitted → received → verified → assigned. Admin handles the first three transitions. Then Dispatch Ops takes over.
📊
All actions are logged with timestamps in the audit trail. Who verified, when, and what unit was assigned — all recorded.

"Dispatch Now" sends the mission. This changes status to "dispatched" and the Rescue Team is immediately notified on their dashboard.
📋
4 Tabs:
• Pending — verified incidents waiting to be dispatched
• Active — dispatched/en-route missions (monitoring)
• To Close — resolved by rescue team, awaiting your confirmation
• Closed — completed case log
❗
Do NOT dispatch without a verified incident. Only incidents that have been verified by Admin appear in your Pending tab.
✓
Dispatcher's final job: After rescue team marks "resolved," the case appears in "To Close." Click "Confirm & Close Case" to officially close it.

rescue-portal.ph/staff-portal
📊
Staff Portal
Read Only
👁️ Read-only view. You can monitor all incidents but cannot modify statuses.
⚠️
Only Dispatch Ops can close cases. The "Confirm & Close Case" button is the FINAL step. Once closed, the incident is archived permanently.
⏱️
Full timeline is shown: Reported → Dispatched → Arrived → Resolved timestamps. This is the audit trail for accountability and reporting.
✓
After closing: Status → "closed." The case moves to the "Closed" tab as a historical log. All roles can see it in their respective dashboards.
📊
Staff Portal (shown below) is purely observational — staff can see all incidents and their statuses but have NO action buttons. Used for monitoring/reporting.
❗
Do NOT close a case prematurely. Verify with the rescue team that the situation is fully resolved before clicking close. This prevents reopening issues later.
